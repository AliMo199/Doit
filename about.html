Dark mode support offers users an alternative visual theme that reduces the overall brightness of the app interface. About

In today’s digital age, maintaining productivity and organizing tasks has become essential for everyone, from busy professionals to students. However, I found that many existing to-do list applications and websites often come with unnecessary complications. When you search for a simple to-do list tool on Google, you are bombarded with options that require you to sign up, log in, and navigate through a plethora of features that you may not need. This can be overwhelming and counterproductive for users who just want a straightforward way to manage their daily tasks.

This realization inspired me to create a more user-friendly to-do list application. My goal was to develop a tool that is simple, intuitive, and accessible without the need for cumbersome registration processes. By focusing on the core functionalities—adding, completing, and deleting tasks, along with offering a dark mode for ease of use at night—I aimed to create an application that truly meets users' needs without any unnecessary hurdles.

Moreover, as a student in Computer Science, I have always been passionate about developing practical applications that can make everyday tasks easier. During my studies, one of the first projects we were assigned was to implement a to-do list using various programming languages such as C++. This early exposure to building a to-do list application not only helped me understand the fundamental concepts of programming but also sparked my interest in creating software solutions that enhance productivity.

Combining these experiences, my project, DOIT, was born. It is a simple yet powerful tool designed to help users organize their tasks efficiently, without the need for complex sign-up processes. This project reflects my journey in computer science and my desire to create solutions that are both practical and user-centric.
